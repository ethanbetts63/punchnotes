
Kill Tony timeline from launch to June 14 2026
Executive summary
Kill Tony began as a Deathsquad pilot recorded on June 3, 2013 and posted on June 18, 2013 with Tony Hinchcliffe, Brian Redban, and Rick Ingraham. By episode #16, recorded September 16, 2013 and posted October 9, 2013, the Deathsquad archive was already using the Kill Tony name, with early recurring onstage figures including Iron Patriot, Sara Mostajabi, and Kim Congdon; Sara Weinshenk is in the credited rotation by episodes #24-25 in November 2013. The show’s early long-run base was the Comedy Store in Los Angeles, then it pivoted into a pandemic “Quarantined” run in 2020, relocated to Austin with episode #487 in January 2021, and settled into its current regular home at Comedy Mothership with episode #602 recorded March 13, 2023. 
The show’s scale changed dramatically after the Austin move. The 10-year anniversary was taped at ACL Live on June 10, 2023 as episode #616. The first clearly documented arena step-up was the sold-out H-E-B Center New Year’s run on December 30-31, 2023 for episodes #647 and #648. In 2024 the show jumped from a sold-out YouTube Theater booking in Los Angeles to an added sold-out Kia Forum date, then reached Madison Square Garden on August 9-10, 2024, which Pollstar reported sold 24,228 tickets for $2.39 million gross. 
The business/distribution story is also visible. The show started on Deathsquad, had a dedicated Kill Tony YouTube channel by December 15, 2015, announced a Studio71 partnership on March 7, 2024, announced a Netflix three-special deal on March 10, 2025, then added KillTonyMania on Netflix in 2026 and an exclusive Red Seat Ventures multiyear partnership on April 28, 2026. As of June 14, 2026, the latest official episode I could verify is KT #771, listed on the Deathsquad home feed on June 8, 2026 and in the official embedded YouTube feed on June 9, 2026, recorded May 18, 2026. 
The biggest documented show-linked controversy was the Peng Dang incident in May 2021, after which Antone’s publicly said it was no longer working with Tony and Vulture reported that WME had dropped him and two Joe Rogan live dates removed him. The other major host-level spillover event was Tony’s October 27, 2024 joke about Puerto Rico at Donald Trump’s Madison Square Garden rally, followed by a public no-apology response on X. On the human side of the show, Michael Lehrer — one of the defining recurring regulars of the Austin period — was diagnosed with ALS in 2017 and died on January 17, 2023, aged 44. 
Scope and evidence
This report uses recording dates first when the official Deathsquad episode page supplies them, and post/upload dates second when only publication timing is available. That matters because many major Kill Tony episodes were posted days or weeks after they were taped. When an exact date could not be pinned from the available source set, it is marked “unspecified.” 
Primary and near-primary sources used here are the Deathsquad archive, the official Kill Tony YouTube feed and playlists, official venue pages or venue news items, official company press releases from Studio71, Netflix, and Red Seat Ventures, plus a small number of reputable contemporaneous news reports for controversies and commercial scale. Where those sources did not cleanly expose an exact first/last appearance or an exact venue changeover date, I do not infer beyond the documented minimum.

Open questions and limitations
Some specific items remain partially unresolved in the source set I used. The most important are the exact internal Comedy Store room history, the exact handoff date from Antone’s to Vulcan, and the absolute first appearances for a few regulars where Deathsquad’s readily retrievable tag pages only exposed the earliest credited appearance I could verify, not necessarily the first minute they ever did on the show. 
I also did not elevate every fan-famous awkward episode into the controversies table unless I found a clean contemporaneous source trail strong enough to support a date-precise claim. That means the report is strongest on launch dates, episode dates, venue moves, lineup credits, business deals, large-scale venue economics, public statements, and the Peng Dang / MSG-rally backlash cycles.
